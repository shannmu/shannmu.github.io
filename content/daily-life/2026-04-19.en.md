title = "How to Escape Shutter Island"
date = 2026-04-19
+++

Today, I watched *Shutter Island* with nutt. The story kept bringing me back to
one question: "When I have been labeled as insane, how can I prove that I am
not?"

Here, being labeled is not simply a psychiatrist's diagnosis. It is society's
judgment on an individual.

Once that judgment takes effect, anything the "mad person" says or does can be
marked with the same label of madness. How can someone clear their name when
the accusation has already become an original sin?

Extending this to more kinds of social judgment, the same pattern seems to
exist: once a person is judged, that "original sin" spreads across the whole
self, and any attempt to clear the charge begins to look like an escape from
the judgment itself.
